I am an economist researching environmental economics and industrial organization at the University of Colorado Boulder.

My job market paper, Gone with the Wind: Consumer Surplus from Renewable Generation, uses micro-data from a large wholesale electricity market in the United States to quantify the consumer benefit from renewable generation, in particular wind turbines. I find that the potential consumer benefit is quite large, 50 to 60 USD per person per year. However, less than 70% of this benefit is realized because some electricity generators have an incentive to behave less competitively when wind turbines are generating electricity.
