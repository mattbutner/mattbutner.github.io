Hello! Welcome to my corner of the internet.

I am PhD Candidate at the University of Colorado Boulder Economics Department researching environmental economics and industrial organization. I am on the job market in 2018-2019, and am availble for interviews at the ASSA Annual Meeting in Atlanta.


My job market paper, Gone With The Wind: Consumer Surplus From Renewable Generation, uses micro-data a large wholesale electricity market in the United States to quantify the consumer benefit from renewable generation, in particular wind turbines. I find that this benefit is potentially quite large, 50 to 60 USD per person per year. However, only a 70% of this is realized because electricity generator's have an incentive to act less competitivly when wind turbines are generating electricity. This is because wholesale electricity markets are structured as a multi-unit uniform price auction, and electricity generated from wind turbines is always inframarginal.
